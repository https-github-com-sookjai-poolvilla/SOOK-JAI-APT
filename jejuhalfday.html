Jeju Half Day Tour

Jeju Half Day Tour

1) Hallim Park -> 2) Hyeopjaegul Cave -> 3) O’Sulloc Green Tea Museum This itinerary is perfect for someone who doesn’t want to spend all day and wants to spend about 5-6 hours exploring Jeju.

Hallim Park

At 100,000 square meters, Hallim Park is massive. With all kinds of plants and trees, Hallim Park is a beautiful destination year round. Suggested Tour duration: 1 to 1.5 hours Visit our info page for more information about Hallim Park: Visit our info page for more information about Seongsan Ichulbong Peak:

Hyeopjaegul Cave

Hyeopjaegul Cave is one of several lava tube caves located on the island of Jeju. The lava tubes are thought to have been formed by the lava that created Mt. Hallasan over 25 million years ago. Suggested Tour duration: 1 to 1.5 hours Visit our info page for more information about Hyeopjaegul Cave:

O’Sulloc Tea Museum

O’Sulloc Tea Museum is dedicated to celebrating the history of tea in South Korea. There is also a cafe and a beautiful tea garden. Suggested Tour duration: 1 to 1.5 hours Visit our info page for more information about O’Sulloc Tea Museum

Jeju Horse Park

Jeju’s horse riding culture goes back hundreds of years. Visit Jeju Horse Park and ride a native Jeju horse. There is also a horse museum dedicated to the history of horses in Jeju. Suggested Tour duration: 1 to 1.5 hours Visit our info page for more information about Jeju Horse Park:

Dongmun Traditional Market

Dongmun Traditional Market was open shortly after South Korea’s liberation in 1945. It serves as the oldest permanent market in Jeju. You can purchase everything from street food to clothes. Suggested Tour duration: 1 to 1.5 hours Visit our info page for more information about Dongmun Traditional Market:
